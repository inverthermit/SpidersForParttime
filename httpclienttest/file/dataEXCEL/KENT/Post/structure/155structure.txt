Course structure


 This programme runs on a part-time basis, with formal teaching accompanied by tutor group sessions, action-learning sets, e-learning, e-mentoring and self-directed learning.

Core  modules:

 Research Skills
 Dissertation


Optional modules (subject to availability) include:

 Appraisal of Dental Practices
 Clinical Education
 Clinical Endodontics
 Clinical Minor Oral Surgery
 Clinical Periodontics
 Law and Ethics in Practice
 Leadership and Management  in Primary Care.

   Assessment

  
   Assessment is by a range of methods including:

 clinical key skills portfolio
 clinical case studies
 practice audit
 the completion of an electronic personal development portfolio.

  

 
   Programme aims

  
   The programme aims to:

 provide a structured postgraduate academic training for students in Year 2 of foundation dentist training and within primary and secondary care practice that is designed to enable students to combine academic study with clinical commitment through practice- (work-) related learning
 develop your critical analysis and evaluation skills and appraisal of best practice, through a variety of learning and assessment formats, including enhancing and improving professional practice and ultimately the quality of patient care
 create a supportive and challenging environment in which you can enhance your skills of reflective practice
 develop your critical understanding of professional ethics that apply to and exist within primary and secondary dental care
 facilitate an ethos of academic understanding combined with a career pathway demonstrating the need for lifelong learning and continuing professional development, thereby improving the quality of patient care through practice-based learning
 enable you to work independently and within a team to tackle complex issues and problems and act autonomously in resolving professional problems
 enable you to develop a comprehensive and current knowledge of common clinical dental issues and appropriate sources of referral within primary and secondary dental care
 facilitate the enhancement of a variety of skills in clinical and non-clinical disciplines at M level
 develop your research skills in order to advance evidenced-informed practice within a practice-based dental care setting.

  

 
   Learning outcomes

  
   Knowledge and understanding

   You gain knowledge and understanding of:

 the bases which underpin professional practice roles within dentistry, such as patient management, examination, diagnosis and treatment interventions, collaborative working, mentorship, and education
 generating differential diagnosis and a treatment/management plan based on enhanced understanding of evidence, clinical findings, treatment interventions, with appropriate referral for cases beyond your own competency framework*
 research skills and dissertation project management **
 current best practice in legislation, regulation, ethics, leadership and healthcare management
 dental diseases in populations, health promotion, associated risk factors, preventative programmes and other emerging issues related to public health
 the competencies and ethical responsibilities of working as a regulated professional within a dental team
 experiential learning, personal reflection, mentorship, continuous professional education and development; theories and concepts, and how they apply to clinical and non-clinical situations.


*   relevant for those taking the clinical modules only
** relevant for those intending to proceed to MSc level only
  

 
   
   Intellectual skills

   You develop intellectual skills in the following:

 working independently, efficiently and professionally within frameworks and professional codes of practice and conduct, with recognition of moral and ethical issues and consideration while managing any conflicting priorities
 responsibility for your lifelong learning and continuing professional development, including selected new skills in principles and practice involved in collaborative working, leadership styles and self-management and support of other colleagues and members of the dental team
 collecting and critically analysing data from evidence bases and reflective personal  practice to inform evidence-based enquiry, collecting and analysing qualitative or quantitative data to inform evidence-based enquiry
 designing, implementing, analysing and presenting a research project dissertation.**


** relevant for those intending to proceed to MSc level only
  

 
   
   Subject-specific skills

   You gain subject-specific skills in the following:

 the critical ability to perform patient examination and diagnosis to a level of best practice*
   the critically informed confidence to practice autonomously with competency in selected areas of clinical and non-clinical dentistry, including treatment planning, patient management, medical and dental emergencies, health promotion and disease prevention, collaborative working, mentorship and education; all of these incorporating clinical governance and evidence-based guidelines
 understanding and evaluating the practical application of theoretical learning and evidence base to clinical practice-based learning, and advancing practise in required aspects of primary and secondary care dental practice
 understanding the importance of research and evidence-based techniques to support selected roles and function within a dental healthcare delivery unit such as: mentor, educator, leader and manager of a collaborative team, and the delivery of treatment outcomes and associated measurement
 understanding the methodology related to practice-based research and evidence- based techniques common in primary and secondary dental care settings**


*relevant for those taking the clinical modules only
** relevant for those intending to proceed to MSc level only

  

 
   
   Transferable skills

   You gain the following transferable skills:

 a practical understanding of the need to communicate appropriately and ethically with a range of individuals including patients and relatives, and the community health context; the clinical team, peers and other professionals
 a capacity for reflective autonomous learning and the ability to make   sound judgements and decisions in relationship to complex issues and unpredictable situations using a variety of resources including: literature, evidence bases and feedback
 an ability to develop problem solving skills to a high level of professionalism and ethical standing through learning and reflection
 IT skills including the ability to search for, manage and critically evaluate internet-based information/resources
 a capacity to work effectively within a multi professional environment.
