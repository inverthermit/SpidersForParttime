King's reviews its optional modules on a regular basis, in order to continue to offer innovative and exciting programmes. Therefore, we cannot guarantee that any particular optional course will run in a given year and the options listed below are subject to change.
   
  
     
    YEAR 1
  If you have an A-level in Latin and/or Greek, you will continue your study of the language at an intermediate level, and your first year of study will encompass:
Three compulsory modules:
Working with Greek   Latin Literary Texts: An Introduction
Beginning Greek language
 or Intermediate Greek Languag
e, depending on your language experience
Beginning Latin language
 or Intermediate Latin Language
, depending on your language experience
Choice of one optional module which may include options such as::
Art   Archaeology of Greece   Rome
Introduction to Ancient History (c. 1200 BC-AD 600)
Introduction to Ancient Philosophy

If you have never studied an ancient language before, you will start both Latin and Greek from scratch in year 1, and will take a 3-week intensive summer school module in one language at King&rsquo;s in the summer between years 1 and 2 (Pathway A
). Your first year of study will thus be spread over a longer period than your peers&rsquo;, but will encompass the same overall number of credits:
Five compulsory modules:
Working with Greek   Latin Literary Texts: An Introduction
 Beginning Greek language
Beginning Latin language
Introduction to the Study of Greek and Latin
Summer School Intermediate Latin
 OR Summer School Intermediate Greek

A complete list of modules and descriptions can be found on our website:
http://www.kcl.ac.uk/artshums/depts/classics/modules/index.aspx
 
     
    
  
    
  
   
   
   
   
  
     
  
    
    YEAR 2
   Second year students take at least half their modules in Latin and Greek language or modules with a close reading of texts, such as:
Intermediate  Greek Language
Intermediate  Latin Language
Introduction to Latin Prose Texts
 (Various texts from year to year, past texts have included  Apuleius's Metamorphoses
,  Cicero's Pro Archia Poeta
)
Introduction to Latin Verse Texts
 (Various texts from year to year, past texts have included  Virgil's Georgics
,  Ovid's Heroides
)
Introduction to Greek Prose Texts
 (Various texts from year to year, past texts have included Lucian
,  Herodotus' History
)
Introduction to Greek Verse Texts
 (Various texts from year to year, past texts have included  Aeschylus' Prometheus Bound
,  selections from the tragedies of Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Euripides)

And can choose from a range of optional modules, which may include options such as:
Art and Power in the Age of Alexander
Democracy, Empire and War: Greece 446-338 BC
Grand Tour: Antiquities in London from the Enlightenment to the Present Day
Pompeii: History and Society
Who Needs Myth?
The Trial and Death of Socrates

A complete list of modules and descriptions can be found on our website:
http://www.kcl.ac.uk/artshums/depts/classics/modules/index.aspx
