Students take 360 credits (180 ECTS equivalent).
Currently, students study the following core modules (all are 30 credits unless otherwise indicated). If there are option modules available the current choices are also shown. King's reviews its optional modules on a regular basis, in order to continue to offer innovative and exciting programmes and this list is therefore subject to change. Please check here for updates, or contact the Department for further advice. Students must take and pass 120 credits each year. All compulsory modules and core modules must be taken and passed.
  
  
   
  
     
    YEAR 1
  In Year One students take compulsory modules worth 120 credits:
&bull; Introduction to Development Studies
&bull; History of the Global Economy
&bull; Economic Analysis of Emerging Economies
&bull; Social and Political Analysis of Emerging Economies
 
     
      
  
    
   
  YEAR 1 OPTIONS
   n/a   
  
   
   
   
   
  
     
  
    
    YEAR 2
   In Year Two students take compulsory modules worth 60 credits:
&bull; Development Theory
&bull; Approaches to Researching Development: Quantitative and Qualitative Methods
...and at least 30 credits worth of modules from the following list:
&bull; Diversity and Change in Political Economy of East Asia (15 credits)
&bull; China and Development (15 credits)
&bull; Transition, Emergence   Development in Africa (15 credits)
&bull; South Asian Political Economy (15 credits)
&bull; Political Economy of Development in Latin America (15 credits)
Students may take more credits from the above list or take their remaining 30 credits from non-regional modules such as Migration and Development and Federalism, Decentralization and Development or modules from King&rsquo;s Geography and Political Economy Departments (subject to availability).
 
   
        
  
  
  
 
 
   
    
   
   
   
   
   
   
   YEAR 3
   In Year Three students write an 8-10,000 word dissertation (compulsory) worth 30 credits and take a range of optional modules worth 90 credits in total:
&bull; Gender   Development
&bull; Politics of Market Reforms
&bull; National Development Strategies
&bull; Poverty, Inequality   Social Policy
&bull; Analysing Politics
&bull; Business management in emerging economies
&bull; Health and Development in Emerging Economies
&bull; Poverty and Privilege in the Developing World: Examining Representations of Race and Class in the U.S and Brazil.
&bull; Selected options in Departments of Geography and Political Economy (subject to availability and reciprocal arrangements)
