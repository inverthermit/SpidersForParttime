The degree aims to equip you to discover and apply Psychology as a science. So, it will be taught using dynamic, demanding and interactive methods including problem based learning. 
Please find below an indication of what modules will be offered.  King's reviews its modules on a regular basis, in order to continue to offer innovative and exciting programmes and this list is therefore subject to change. Please check here for updates, or contact the School/department for further advice. 
Students take courses worth 120 credits in each of the three years.   
  
     
    YEAR 1
  25% of the year is spent studying Research Methodology, including the understanding research design, research techniques, and identifying problems with research. Other modules cover:
- Addressing World Issues
- Inspirational Research
- Biological Psychology and Neuropsychology
- Social   Critical Psychology
- Developmental   Cognitive Psychology
- Individual Differences, Personality   Behaviour
 
     
    
  
    
  
   
   
   
   
  
     
  
    
    YEAR 2
   25% of the year is spent studying Research Methodology, including analysis techniques for quantitative and qualitative data, and preparation for the final year project. Other modules cover:
- Philosophy of Mind
- Cognitive Psychology
- Genetics, Neuropsychology and Psychopharmacology
- Social Psychology
- Health Psychology
- Developmental Psychology
- Individual Differences and Personality
- Psychological Skills 
   
      
  
  
  
 
 
  
   
   
   
   
   
   
   YEAR 3
   50% of the final year is spent on a Research project. In addition, students can select modules which cover a broad range of topics from across psychological sciences, including both specialised courses in topics such as neuroscience and integrative broad-based modules.
