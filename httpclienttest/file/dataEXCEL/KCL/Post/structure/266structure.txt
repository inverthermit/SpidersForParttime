STRUCTURE OVERVIEW
     
  Core programme content
   Students take the interdisciplinary team-taught compulsory module &lsquo;Modernity and its Discontents: Society and Culture in the Nineteenth Century&rsquo;, which introduces students to a range of topics and concepts based on themes such as &ldquo;Nation, State and empire&rdquo;, &ldquo;the country and the city&rdquo; and &ldquo;representing the nineteenth century&rdquo;.

   FORMAT AND ASSESSMENT
  Full-time study: 4-8 hours of taught classes per week. Part-time study: 2-6 hours of taught classes per week. The taught compulsory and optional modules are assessed by coursework and/or take-home examination. The compulsory 15,000 word dissertation enables students to research a topic of their choice, working one-to-one with an academic supervisor.
