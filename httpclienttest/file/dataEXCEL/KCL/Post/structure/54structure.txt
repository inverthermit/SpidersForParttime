   Indicative non-core content
   Students take a total of 120 credits from a large range of modules covering Greek and Latin Language acquisition, Greek and Roman thought and literature, ancient history, art and archaeology, and Late Antique and Byzantine Studies. Apart from language acquisition, all modules come from the undergraduate (BA) offering (Levels 4, 5, 6). You are limited to a total of 30 credits from Levels 4 and 5. Only language acquisition (Greek and Latin) is taken at Level 7. The dissertation is optional for all students.
     
   
   FORMAT AND ASSESSMENT
  Modules are largely assessed by coursework but some, such as languages, have in-class tests and end-of-year examinations.
