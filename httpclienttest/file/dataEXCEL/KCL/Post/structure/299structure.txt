      Movement And Exercise - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
PMPTF107    
  
    Credit level:  
4  
    Credit value:  
30    
    
      Semester:  
    Full-year      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: To develop foundation knowledge of the physiology underpinning movement and exercise. To develop an understanding of the mechanisms of movement and the consequences of exercise. To develop skills in the analysis of movement and prescription of exercise that will permit selection, implementation and progression of basic exercise protocols.
Learning Outcomes: Students will be able to demonstrate a basic understanding of how movement occurs; demonstrate an understanding of different types of exercise and their effect on the various body systems; be able to design safe and effective exercise programmes for different purposes; be able to teach and corrcet exercises to individuals and groups; be able to analyse simple movements to detect normal and abnormal patterns; gain a basic understanding of pain and its effect on movement and its management; be able to safely and effectively prescribe aids to movement including walking sticks and wheel chairs; gain a basic knowledge of fitness and its role in preventative strategies in general health management; be able to use the knowledge of the life cycle from birth to old age to inform their selection, prescription and progression of exercises.

  
    Physiotherapy Practice - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
7MPTM043    
  
    Credit level:  
7  
      
    
      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: Initially to extend and develop students&rsquo; knowledge of the physiotherapist&rsquo;s role and skills within core areas of practice (cardio-respiratory, musculoskeletal, and neurology) then to integrate this and evidence-based practice in order to understand the management for patients presenting with complex and multi-factorial clinical conditions.
Also to advance students&rsquo; understanding of current government legislation and the implication of this on their practice in the context of multidisciplinary/interdisciplinary team working and holistic care in meeting the needs of individual clients.
Core Learning Outcomes- students should be able to:
Consistently identify, understand, and explain the problems experienced by individuals with a musculoskeletal, neurological, or cardiorespiratory impairment and how this influences clinical practice
Using an analytical, evidence based approach to clinical problem solving to understand the assessment procedures, evaluation measures and key physiotherapeutic skills commonly used in these core areas of practice and discuss them in the light of current research
Reflect on clinical practice and critically evaluate interventions within contemporary healthcare frameworks

  
    Reflections on Current Practice 1 - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
7MPTM041    
  
    Credit level:  
7  
    Credit value:  
15    
    
      Semester:  
    Full-year      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: This module aims to enable students to develop self-awareness for clinical reasoning and problem-solving in clinical practice by encouraging critical analysis of practice situations. This is achieved through a process of determining how new situations relate to previous knowledge and experience and integrating new knowledge appropriately and productively in a biopsychosocial model of practice.
Core Learning Outcomes &ndash; students should be able to:
Review practice experiences in a way that permits identification of the need for personal development and/or practice change
Engage in a process of practice consideration that involves systematic critical enquiry and a willingness to synthesise evidence from a variety of sources leading to enhanced understanding of practice
Describe principle methods of human communication and theories relating to teaching, learning and information giving to predict barriers to communication with both clients and professional colleagues and how to respond appropriately
Discuss various theories of human development and evaluate the ways in which they help us to understand human behaviour and reaction in times of health, illness, challenge and stress
Consider the need for psychological care in various client circumstances and practice contexts and evaluate its impact on client/therapist relationships, client experiences of physiotherapy, empowerment of clients
Reflect on ethical, moral and legal issues encountered in clinical practice and the need to promote dignity in care

  
    Reflections on Current Practice 2 - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
7MPTM042    
  
    Credit level:  
7  
    Credit value:  
30    
    
      Semester:  
    Full-year      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: This module aims to provide students with the opportunities and skills to examine and critically analyse the role and efficacy of Physiotherapy within the wider multiprofessional landscape of the changing health and social care environment. In particular to consider whether and how practitioners and services provide quality, evidence-based, effective and importantly, client-centred services that are responsive to individuals needs and the development of social policy.
The module aims to do this by maximizing students learning from practice and enabling them to identify appropriate and realistic personal and professional goals for Continuing Personal and Professional Development (CPPD) and lifelong learning.
Core Learning Outcomes &ndash; students should be able to:
Critically evaluate and debate the complexity of the health care system within which physiotherapy is delivered
Critically analyse the effectiveness and appropriateness of innovative service delivery initiatives in delivering an effective service that is tailored to the needs and wishes of individual patients
Reflect on ethical, moral and legal issues encountered in clinical practice and use this to maximize the opportunities for learning from practicework effectively as both an independent practitioner and collaboratively within a multiprofessional team

  Aims: This module aims to provide the students with:
The scientific basis for rehabilitation and exercise programmes in a wide variety of individuals including preventative exercise in healthy individuals

    Research Project- Physiotherapy - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
7MPTM020    
  
    Credit level:  
7  
      
    
      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  The research project takes the form of either a short scientific investigation or a systematic literature review. Both of these are carried out under supervision of a member of academic staff.
The experimental studies will use qualitative (psychosocial) or quantitative methodologies and in some cases a combination of the two. They may consist of original work with established methodologies or techniques, the development of new techniques/tools, or appropriate replication of previous work.
Students are encouraged to work in small groups but must analyse their data and write the dissertation independently.
Aims: To give the student the opportunity to address in depth a topic of particular interestTo train and give the student experience in practical research skills including data analysis and interpretationTo assess the student with respect to their ability to plan, implement and report research work
Core learning outcomes - students should be able to;
Identify a feasible research project with the assistance of their supervisor
Master the techniques and skills appropriate for their project
Analyse their data with relevant statistical techniques
Interpret their data in the light of existing literature
Write a dissertation about their study in the form of a published paper

  
    Understanding Cardiorespiratory Problems - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
PMPTF109    
  
    Credit level:  
4  
    Credit value:  
15    
    
      Semester:  
    Semester 2 (spring)      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: To introduce students to the physiotherapy problems of the patient affected by cardiovascular or respiratory disease. To provide students with an understanding of the normal structure and function of the heart, lungs and chest wall. To introduce students to the normal physiology of the cardiovascular and respiratory systems. To introduce students to the pathophysiology of common cardiorespiratory diseases, providing a foundation for the understanding of physiotherapy problems and their management.
Learning Outcomes: On completion of the course the students will: demonstrate knowledge of the phsyiology of the cardiovascular and respiratory systems; have acquired the ability to perform laboratory experiments in cardiovascular and respiratory phsyiology; have an understanding of the anatomy of the heart, lungs and chest wall and will be able to identify the surface markings of the lungs, pleura and heart; have an understanding of the physiotherapy problems of the patient with cardiorespiratory disease and be able to relate them to changes in normal phsyiology and/or anatomy; have a basic understanding of the impact of the above problems on the patient's activity, participation and quality of life; have a basic understanding of how phsyiotherapy may help to alleviate or ameliorate client problems or respiratory origin.

  
    Understanding Musculoskeletal Problems - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
PMPTF108    
  
    Credit level:  
4  
    Credit value:  
30    
    
      Semester:  
    Full-year      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  
Aims: To introduce students to the basic theory and skills underpinning physiotherapy practice.   To integrate peripheral anatomical knowledge with assessment of client needs and evaluation procedures.   To introduce students to assessment, goal setting and evaluation of practice in the phsyiotherapy management of clinical problems.   To acquaint the student with the role and scope of physiotherapy in health care and promotion within a multiprofessional context.   To foster an evidence-based approach to the management of patients with peripheral musculoskeletal problems.
Learning Outcomes: On completion of this module the student should be able to: explain the pathological processes, aetiology and overall management of a range of conditions affecting the peripheral musculoskeletal system; demonstrate how anatomical knowledge can be used to interpret the assessment and re-assessment findings; demonstrate basic clinical decision making skills in developing assessments and treatment plans; demonstrate a basic understanding of the impact of peripheral muscuoskeletal problems on the patient's activity, participation and quality of life; demonstrate safe and effective use of manual therapies with evidence based decision making and attempt at evaluation of the outcome; justify the efficient use and safe application of electrotherapeutic modalities; carry out and rationalise the phsyiotherapeutic skills taught in the context of patient management; demonstrate an awareness of current research relevant to patient care and physiotherapy management; recognise the need for patient confidentiality and non-discriminatory practice in the individualised care of patients; recognise the legal and professional 'duty of care' towards patients and the need to keep full, clear and securely held written records.

  
    Understanding Neurological Problems - Required

   
      
  
    
      
     Module code:  
PMPTF110    
  
    Credit level:  
4  
    Credit value:  
15    
    
      Semester:  
    Full-year      
   
    
  
       
   
    
    
    
     
  Aims: To introduce students to the basic principles of neurophysiology. To develop an awareness of models of health, illness and disability and their relevance in addressing the needs of and working positively with people with problems associated with neurological dysfunction. To provide the opportunity for students to practice basic neurological physiotherapy skills and discuss their role in relation to current research. To facilitate students' understanding of assessment procedures and outcome measures used in neurological physiotherapy practice.
Learning Outcomes: On completion of the course the students should be able to: demonstrate an understanding of basic neurophysiology concepts; models of health, illness and disability; acute chronic and deteriorating conditions; recognise and describe common neurological signs and symptoms in different client groups; begin to link the above signs and symptoms with functional problems encountered by clients with problems of neurological origin; understand the need for clinical assessment in neurological physiotherapy; demonstrate appropriate skills concerning sensory and deep tendon reflex testing, positioning, analysis of movement, assisting with transfers, and balance re-education for the patient with neurological dysfunction.
