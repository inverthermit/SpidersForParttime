    Behavioural Finance - Optional

    
  
      
     Module code:  
7SSMM610    
  
    Credit level:  
7  
    Credit value:  
15  
  
  
      Semester:  
    Semester 2 (spring)     
   
    
  
      Assessment:  
      written examination/s;     coursework;     
      2 hour Exam (70%); 2 lab assignments (30%)
     
  By the end of the module,
students will be able to demonstrate systematic understanding of the technical tools required to analyze issues in behavioural finance, such as game theoretical concepts and advanced statistical analysis, and relate them to other concepts from accounting and financial management;
students will be able to show a systematic understanding of key behavioural concepts such as crowding, herding, risk biases, overconfidence, noise, deception, as applied to financial environments, and to exemplify such concepts;
students will be able to critically evaluate normative theories such as the efficient market hypothesis (EMH), and will be able to design tools and experiments falsifying the main tenets of the EMH;
students will be able to demonstrate sound comprehensive knowledge of how social behaviour influences decision making processes in financial markets, and apply this knowledge to financial decision making situations;
students will be able to accurately interpret financial models with a view to their behavioural foundations, and analyze the implications thereof;
students will be able to analyse and evaluate the outcomes of experimental games, interpret them in a theoretically informed context and derive consequences for practical decision making situations.
